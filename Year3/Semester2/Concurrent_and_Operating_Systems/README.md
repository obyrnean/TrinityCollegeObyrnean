This repository contains the coursework, labs, and assignments for the **Concurrent and Operating Systems** module. The focus was on understanding the structure and behavior of operating systems, and how concurrency is handled through threads, synchronization primitives, and scheduling algorithms.

The course explored the internals of process management, memory, and synchronization, as well as hands-on implementation of multithreaded programs using POSIX threads in C.

---

##  Topics Covered
- **Operating System fundamentals**
- **Processes vs Threads**
- **POSIX Threads (pthreads)**
- **Thread creation and termination**
- **Race conditions and data sharing**
- **Mutexes, semaphores, and condition variables**
- **Deadlocks and starvation**
- **Scheduling algorithms (FCFS, SJF, Round Robin, etc.)**
- **Producer-Consumer, Readers-Writers problems**
- **Critical sections and synchronization**
